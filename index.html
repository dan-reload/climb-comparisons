Training Priorities for May

Benwerrin is the event climb. It has the steepest gradient (18%) and that final 1 km kick comes when you're already fatigued from 11 km of climbing. This is what you need to prepare for.

Cootha (6.73 km, 467 m): Use this for building sustained power. The 2.2 km at 9.2% teaches your body to maintain threshold effort on moderate-steep climbing. Good for VO2Max work and power development.

Nebo (15.6 km, 1,091 m): Use this for endurance and pacing discipline. It's nearly 2.3x longer than Benwerrin and teaches you how to manage fuel and effort over a long climb with multiple hard pitches. Great for race simulation.

The key difference: Benwerrin's 18% finish is nastier than anything Cootha or Nebo throw at you individually. Your May prep should include regular work on that final-kilometre power when fatigued. Train Cootha for power foundation, Nebo for endurance foundation, but specifically practise Benwerrin-style finish-with-power efforts regularly.
